1987 Constitution of the Philippines

February 2, 1987

The 1987 Constitution of the Philippines was ratified on February 2, 1987, following the EDSA People Power Revolution that ousted Ferdinand Marcos from power. It replaced the 1973 Constitution, which was associated with Marcos' authoritarian rule during Martial Law. The new Constitution was drafted by a Constitutional Commission appointed by President Corazon Aquino with the goal of restoring democracy, protecting human rights, and preventing the return of dictatorship.
1987

Key features of the 1987 Constitution include: 1. Preamble: It begins with a statement of the Filipino people's aspirations for justice, freedom, and democracy. 2. Declaration of Principles and State Policies: It emphasizes the sovereignty of the people, social justice, and human rights. 3. Bill of Rights: Guarantees fundamental freedoms such as freedom of speech, due process, and protection against abuses by the state. 4. Government Structure: The Constitution divides government into three branches: a. Legislative: Bicameral Congress, consisting of the Senate and the House of Representatives. b. Executive: The President, who serves a six-year term and has limited powers. c. Judiciary: Led by the Supreme Court, with the power of judicial review and judicial independence.
1987

5. Local Government and Autonomy: Grants local governments autonomy, including the creation of regions like the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM). 6. Social Justice and Economic Provisions: Addresses inequalities through policies on land reform, labor rights, and protections for marginalized groups. It also regulates foreign ownership and promotes equitable distribution of resources. 7. National Development: Promotes free education, cultural preservation, and scientific progress to support the country's development. 8. Safeguards against Abuses of Power: Includes term limits for public officials and the establishment of independent institutions to prevent the abuse of power. 9. Amendment and Revision: Specifies procedures for changing or revising the Constitution, with provisions for a peaceful transition from dictatorship to democracy. Overall, the 1987 Constitution provides a framework for democratic governance, ensuring accountability, protection of citizens' rights, and checks and balances to prevent the concentration of power.
